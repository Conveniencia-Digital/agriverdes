Biography

Luo Shiming, professor of Agroecology, South China Agricultural University.

After he finished his undergraduate study on agronomy in South China Agricultural University (SCAU) in 1968, he first worked as an agricultural technician in Agriculture Bureau, Xinyi County, Guangdong Province for 10 years. Then he returned to SCAU for his graduate study. During 1981 to 1982, he studied agroecology in the Institute of Ecology, University of Georgia, USA. After that, he conducted research and teaching on agroecology in SCAU. His main research field include: (1) sustainable agroecosystems, (2) biodiversity in agriculture, (3) allelopathy among agricultural plants, and (4) computer simulation in crop production system. He delivered courses on Agroecology, General Ecology, Advance in Ecology, and Systems Analysis in Ecology. He has trained more than 50 M.S. and Ph.D. students and published more than 160 papers and 14 books.

He was the President of SCAU from 1995 to 2006, and Director of the Institute of Tropical and Subtropical Ecology in South China Agricultural University from 1994 to 2014. He used to be the Vice President of China Agronomy Society, Vice President of Scientists’ Association of Guangzhou, Vice President of China Ecology Society, and Executive Vice President of International Allelopathy Society, President of Asian Allelopathy Society, and member of International Ecology Society.

He got an award from the Ministry of Education as the Outstanding Teacher of Higher Education in China, 2007. He also received his Honorary Doctorial Degree from Pennsylvanian State University, USA at the same year.
